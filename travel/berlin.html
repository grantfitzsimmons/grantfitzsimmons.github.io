Berlin, Germany

Description of Berlin

Germany is a diverse nation with a long and rich history. I chose to study and research it as it has one of the most interesting population makeups, large history of warfare in a global sense, and radical shifts in political power and beliefs over the years.

Berlin is home to world-renowned universities, orchestras, museums, and entertainment venues, and is host to many sporting events. Its urban setting has made it a sought-after location for international film productions. The city is well known for its festivals, diverse architecture, nightlife, contemporary arts and a high quality of living. Since the 2000s Berlin has seen the emergence of a cosmopolitan entrepreneurial scene.

Things to Do

  • Visit the Brandenburg Gate, a classical archway & city divide 
  • Explore the Reichstag building, the historic glass-domed home of parliament
  • Walk around Alexanderplatz, the pedestrianized square with the iconic German tower

History of Berlin

Berlin was founded in the 13th century and became the capital of the Margraviate of Brandenburg in 1417. From there, it became the capital of Brandenburg-Prussia and then the Kingdom of Prussia as the empire expanded. Over the 18th and 19th centuries, Prussia grew and became the early German Empire in 1871. It became a major world city, known for higher education, science, the humanities, music, museums, government, diplomacy and military affairs. It was nearly completely destroyed during World War II and became split between the victors of the war. After Germany was reunified in 1990 it became the capital once again.
